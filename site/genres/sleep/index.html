Audio DNA

Feature Summary

Rhythm & Tonality

BPM Distribution
Sleep music clusters around 60-90 BPM (77%), with the median of 70.2 BPM matching a relaxed heart rate. Some tracks have zero BPM, indicating pure ambient drones without rhythmic pulse.
Key Distribution
C major dominates (19%), followed by C#/Db and D (12% each). Simple, natural keys create the most accessible and peaceful harmonic environments for rest.
Major vs Minor Mode
Overwhelmingly major (71%) vs minor (29%). Unlike other ambient genres, sleep music favors consonant, uplifting harmonies that create safe, comforting sonic spaces.
Duration Histogram
Most tracks are 3-4 minutes (40%), though some extend beyond 8 minutes for deep immersion. The median of 3.3 minutes balances structure with extended calm.

Emotional Fingerprint

Energy vs Valence
Sleep music occupies the extreme low-energy, low-valence quadrant. The target zone shows the genre's precise requirements: virtually no stimulation while maintaining neutral to slightly positive emotional tone.
Acousticness vs Instrumentalness
Extreme concentration in the upper-right quadrant reveals sleep music's commitment to pure, organic instrumental sounds. This combination eliminates any elements that might disrupt rest.

Prompt Template 
Ultra-quiet, drone-like, no rhythmic pulse, sub-60 BPM, whisper-quiet dynamics. Pure ambient texture, possibly binaural beats, extremely long sustains. The goal is to be barely perceptible.

• Minimal piano with infinite sustain (70 BPM or slower)
• Gentle string pads with glacial evolution
• No percussion or rhythmic elements
• Binaural beats or pink/brown noise textures
• Ultra-low dynamics (-40dB), gentle fade-ins
• No sudden changes, extremely slow harmonic movement
• Pure tones, organic resonance only

Artists to reference: Brian Eno, Max Richter "Sleep" project, Marconi Union "Weightless", Ludovico Einaudi ambient works

Duration: 3-8+ minutes for deep immersion cycles
