Rhythm & Tonality

BPM Distribution
Jazz piano spreads across a wide tempo range, with 42% in the 90-120 BPM range and 27% in 120-150 BPM. This distribution reflects the genre's versatility from ballads to medium swing tempos.
Key Distribution
G leads with 15% of tracks, followed by C and G#/Ab (13% and 12%). This distribution shows jazz piano's comfort across all keys, with slight preference for guitar-friendly keys.
Major vs Minor Mode
Major modes dominate at 61%, giving jazz piano a generally optimistic character despite its contemplative nature. The 39% minor content provides emotional depth and complexity.
Duration Histogram

Emotional Fingerprint

Energy vs Valence
Jazz piano clusters in the contemplative low-energy zone (25-40%) with moderate valence spread (15-50%). The target zone captures the genre's introspective yet emotionally nuanced character, perfect for intimate listening.
Acousticness vs Instrumentalness
Strong bias toward high acousticness (75-99%) reflects jazz piano's acoustic nature. Lower instrumentalness (0-40%) shows the genre's vocal tradition, with piano often accompanying singers or serving as lead voice itself.
