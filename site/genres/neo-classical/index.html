Rhythm & Tonality

BPM Distribution
Neo-classical spans a wider tempo range than expected, with the 90-150 BPM range dominating (65%). The median of 109 BPM provides a contemplative yet purposeful pace that allows for both reflection and movement.
Key Distribution
A minor emerges as the emotional center (13%), followed by C and G major (12% each). The prevalence of both major and minor keys reflects the genre's ability to express complex emotions from melancholy to hope.
Major vs Minor Mode
Evenly split at 55% major, 45% minor. Major keys provide uplift and resolution, while minor keys explore deeper emotional territories. This balance creates the genre's characteristic emotional complexity.
Duration Histogram
Most pieces last 2-4 minutes (53%), with a median of 3.3 minutes. This length allows for proper development of musical ideas while maintaining focus - perfect for both concentrated listening and background ambience.

Emotional Fingerprint

Energy vs Valence
Neo-classical occupies the low-energy, low-valence quadrant with remarkable consistency. The target zone highlights the genre's sweet spot: deeply contemplative without being harsh, emotionally profound without overwhelming intensity.
Acousticness vs Instrumentalness
Dense clustering in the upper-right quadrant reveals neo-classical's commitment to organic, instrumental sound. Nearly all tracks prioritize natural acoustics and instrumental expression over electronic production or vocals.

Feature Correlations

Correlation Heatmap

Production Profile

Neo-Classical Production Characteristics
The genre's production philosophy emphasizes natural acoustic recording, dynamic expression, and minimal digital processing. Pedal sustain and orchestral layering create rich harmonic textures while preserving intimate emotional connection.
